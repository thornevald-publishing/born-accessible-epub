What’s Happening

The European Accessibility Act (EAA) is moving from theory to enforcement. The grace period is ending. From 2026 onward, accessibility is no longer optional for ebooks sold in the EU — regardless of the publisher’s home country.

The benchmark is now clear: EPUB 3.3 with declared accessibility metadata, aligned with WCAG-based requirements for digital publications.

This applies to:

  • Indie authors
  • Small and mid-sized presses
  • US-, UK-, AU-, and NZ-based publishers selling into EU storefronts

Retailers, libraries, and public institutions are expected to begin active enforcement through:

  • Upload validation
  • Metadata checks
  • Procurement exclusions
  • Consumer complaints

This is not a format trend. It is a legal compliance shift.
